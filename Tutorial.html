TaleScaper Tutorial

Getting Started

Welcome to TaleScaper, your interactive story creation tool! This tutorial will guide you through the features and functionality of TaleScaper, helping you create engaging, non-linear stories with ease.

Creating Your Story

  1. Start by clicking the "Add New Part" button to create your first story segment.
  2. Each part has a default name (e.g., "Part 1"). You can edit this name by clicking the pencil icon next to it.
  3. Write your story content in the text area provided for each part.
  4. To create choices, click the "Add Choice" button within a story part.
  5. Enter the choice text and select which part it should lead to using the dropdown menu.
  6. To remove a choice, click the "Remove Choice" button next to it.
  7. You can remove an entire story part by clicking the "Remove Part" button at the bottom of each part.

Editing Part Names

Giving your story parts descriptive names can help you navigate your story structure more easily. Here's how to edit part names:

  1. Look for the pencil icon next to the current part name.
  2. Click on the pencil icon to activate the edit mode.
  3. Type in your new part name.
  4. Press Enter or click outside the input field to save the new name.

Using Play Mode

Play Mode allows you to experience your story as a reader would. To use Play Mode:

  1. Click the "Switch to Play Mode" button at the top of the page.
  2. Read through your story and click on choices to navigate through your tale.
  3. Use the progress bar at the top to track your progress.

Saving and Loading Stories

TaleScaper allows you to save your work and load it later:

  • Click the "Save" button to download your story as a JSON file.
  • Use the "Load" button to upload a previously saved story file.
  • Your work is automatically saved in your browser's local storage, so you won't lose progress if you accidentally close the page.

Tips for Great Stories

  • Plan your story structure before you start writing. A flowchart can be helpful!
  • Create meaningful choices that significantly impact the story.
  • Use descriptive part names to help you navigate your story structure.
  • Test your story frequently in Play Mode to ensure all paths work as intended.
  • Consider different endings based on the choices made throughout the story.
